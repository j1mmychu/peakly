# UX Report — 2026-04-07

**Design Score: 6/10**

The app has a solid foundation — card grid looks decent, venue detail sheet has good photo hero, 7-day forecast is well-designed. But there are multiple violations of prior design decisions that erode quality, and several P1 bugs still live that make the app feel unfinished for an April 15 ProductHunt launch.

---

## Top 3 Visual Issues

### 1. Emoji Pollution in UI Chrome (Decision Violation)
The decision "Remove emoji from UI chrome — emoji in category pills, section headers, and badges look amateur. Clean text + SVG only" was made weeks ago and **never enforced**. Emoji are everywhere:

- **Category pills** (line 6879): `{c.emoji} {c.label}` renders snowflake, surfer, beach emoji in nav pills
- **VenueDetailSheet section headers**: "You'd also like" (line 9571), "Insider Tips" (line 9598), "Book an experience" (line 9637) all have emoji
- **Share & Invite button** (line 9459): has clipboard emoji
- **Profile notifications** (lines 8119-8121): fire, plane, calendar emoji
- **Profile CTA** (line 7939): rocket emoji in "Create my adventure profile"

**Impact:** Makes the app look like a side project, not a premium travel product. Airbnb, KAYAK, AllTrails — none use emoji in UI chrome.

### 2. Stowe Mountain Resort Shows the Matterhorn
The hero photo for "Stowe Mountain Resort, Vermont, USA" is clearly the Matterhorn (Switzerland). This is a trust-destroying visual error. If venue photos are incorrect, users won't trust the data either. This ties into the P1 venue data quality issue (expansion venue photo duplication — 43% of 1,500 expansion venues share ~90 images).

### 3. GO/MAYBE/WAIT Badges Are Too Aggressive on Cards
The orange "MAYBE" and red "WAIT" badges on every card in the grid create a wall of warning colors. When most venues show MAYBE or WAIT (which is the natural state for most venues most of the time), the grid looks negative and discouraging. Airbnb doesn't put "MEDIOCRE" badges on listings — it shows them in the best light possible and lets users decide.

## Top 3 Interaction Issues

### 1. Score Vote Buttons Still Rendered (P1 — Lines 9510-9515)
Decision was made 2026-03-25 to CUT thumbs up/down. Still live 13 days later. Lines 9510-9515 render vote buttons in VenueDetailSheet's conditions box. Lines 9305 and 9314-9317 manage state. This was marked as P1 in CLAUDE.md bug #19.

**Fix:** Remove the vote button JSX at lines 9509-9516 and the state/handler at lines 9305, 9314-9323.

### 2. "Checking conditions..." Text Visible on Cards
Several cards in the grid show "Checking conditions..." overlay text on their photos while weather loads. This looks broken rather than loading. The shimmer skeleton loaders exist and are well-designed, but the text overlay on photos with actual images underneath creates a jarring "something is wrong" feeling.

### 3. Email Capture Has No Backend (Line 7214)
The "Notify me" email capture form fires `alert("You're on the list!")` — signups are literally lost. Every email that could convert into a ProductHunt upvote or Reddit user is gone. This is the highest-ROI bug to fix before launch.

## Inspiration

**Steal from Surfline:** Their spot detail page shows a clean condition summary with no clutter — wind arrow, swell height, tide graph. All data-viz, no emoji, no badges, no vote buttons. The data speaks for itself. Peakly's VenueDetailSheet should aspire to that same information density without the visual noise.

---

## Decision Made

**All emoji removed from UI chrome elements.** Category pills, section headers, buttons, badges, and notification labels will use clean text only. Emoji remain acceptable only in venue data fields (icon property) and inline content tips where they serve as visual shorthand (e.g., insider tips emoji bullets). This aligns with the 2026-03-23 decision that was never enforced.

---

## Priority for April 15 ProductHunt Launch

The three issues that will sink the launch if not fixed:

1. **Email capture backend** — every signup is lost (P1, #17 in CLAUDE.md)
2. **Venue dedup + photo quality** — wrong photos destroy trust (P1, #12-13)
3. **Vote buttons removed + emoji stripped** — amateur signals that undercut the "Steve Jobs good" standard

The app is closer than it looks, but these three gaps are the difference between "cool side project" and "I'd pay for this."
