New users land on Explore and see numbers like "Score: 72" with no context. What does 72 mean? What's good? The scoring system is Peakly's core value prop and it's invisible to first-timers. This is the #1 retention risk before Reddit launch.

## Product Decision

**Promoting the scoring system as the hero feature.** Every competitor shows raw conditions data (temp, wind, swell). Peakly's angle is the synthesized score — "is this a good time to go?" That's only compelling if users understand what the number means. The onboarding explainer (priority #2) isn't a nice-to-have, it's the difference between "cool app" and "I get it, I need this."
