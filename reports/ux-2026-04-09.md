## Top 3 Visual Issues

### 1. Emoji plague still infesting UI chrome (P1 — Day 15 unfixed)

Decision to cut emoji was made March 25. It is April 9. Every competitor (Airbnb, AllTrails, KAYAK, Surfline) uses zero emoji in their UI chrome. Peakly has emoji in:

- Category pills: `{c.emoji} {c.label}` at lines 6879, 7471
- VenueDetailSheet section headers: "Insider Tips" (line 9598), "You'd also like" (line 9571), "Book an experience" (line 9637)
- Profile CTA: "Create my adventure profile" (line 7939)
- Share button: "Share Peakly - Invite Friends" (line 8316)
- Notification labels: "Peak conditions", "Flight deals", "Weekly digest" in Profile tab all have emoji prefixes
- Location pins use emoji instead of SVG pin icon

This single issue is the difference between "student project" and "real product." Every surface of the app screams hobby app because of emoji.

### 2. VenueDetailSheet hero photo often fails to load

On Florianopolis detail sheet, the hero area was mostly dark gradient with a faint rainbow photo that barely loaded. The dark overlay `rgba(0,0,0,0.58)` is too aggressive when the photo itself is dark. Airbnb uses a lighter scrim (`rgba(0,0,0,0.25)`) that lets the photo breathe. When photos fail entirely, users see a black void above the venue name.

### 3. "Checking conditions..." state dominates the card grid

Every single CompactCard on the Explore tab displayed "Checking conditions..." with shimmer placeholders. The batched weather fetching (50/batch, 2s delay) means users stare at loading states for 30+ seconds when 2,043 spots are rendering. Cards should show venue name, location, photo, and flight price immediately — conditions can fill in progressively. Right now the loading badge overlays the photo and makes the grid look broken.

## Top 3 Interaction Issues

### 1. Score vote buttons still rendered (lines 9510-9515)

Decision to CUT thumbs up/down was made March 25. They're still live on every VenueDetailSheet. The `handleScoreVote` function (lines 9315-9323) and `scoreVotes` localStorage state (line 9305) are all still wired up. These buttons are confusing — users don't understand what voting on a condition score means. They add visual clutter to the already-dense conditions panel.

**Fix:** Delete lines 9305, 9314-9323, and 9509-9516. Remove the `scoreVotes` state entirely.

### 2. No loading skeleton for hero card

The "YOUR BEST WINDOW RIGHT NOW" hero card at the top of Explore shows Grand Targhee Resort with a WAIT badge and score of 50. When weather hasn't loaded yet, the hero still renders with whatever partial data exists. Should show a full-width shimmer skeleton until at least one scored venue is available, similar to how Airbnb handles its hero.

### 3. Alerts tab empty state is too sparse

The Alerts tab empty state has "No alerts yet" centered text and a single CTA button. Below that is just white space for 60% of the screen. This is a high-value conversion surface — it should show 2-3 example alert cards (grayed out / mock) to demonstrate the value proposition before the user commits. Airbnb's saved search empty state shows what you'd get if you set one up. Peakly's just says "trust us."

## Typography & Color Notes

- Type scale is generally consistent. Plus Jakarta Sans works well. Font weights (400-900) are used appropriately across the hierarchy.
- The `#0284c7` primary blue is clean but overused — it appears on category pills, "Peak" labels, forecast day highlights, and interactive elements. Consider reserving it for interactive elements only and using `#555` or `#222` for informational text.
- Condition score colors (red/orange/gray) at line 9506 create a clear hierarchy. Good.
- The green `#f0fff4` background on the flight price panel is effective — draws attention to the deal. Keep it.
- "POWERED BY" section in Profile uses too many emoji in the API badges. Clean text labels with subtle icons would look more professional.

## Accessibility Concerns

- BottomNav uses proper `aria-label` and `aria-current`. Good.
- Heart button has proper `aria-label` for saved state. Good.
- The "Checking conditions..." overlay text on cards has white text on semi-transparent dark background — contrast may be borderline on lighter photos. Should verify 4.5:1 minimum.
- Range slider for max flight price has no visible label or `aria-label` — just the visual "Max flight price" text above it.

## Inspiration

**Steal from Surfline's forecast bar.** Their 7-day forecast uses colored bars (green/yellow/red) instead of text labels to convey conditions at a glance. Peakly's 7-day forecast in VenueDetailSheet is functional but text-heavy. A horizontal color bar under each day showing condition quality (green = GO, yellow = OKAY, red = WAIT) would communicate the same information in 10% of the visual space and would make the "best window" immediately obvious without reading numbers.
