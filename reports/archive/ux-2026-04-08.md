### 2. "Checking conditions..." Overlay Creates Anxiety

Observed on live site: when you land on Explore, every card in the grid shows "Checking conditions..." as a dark overlay on top of actual venue photos. This creates a double visual — beautiful photo underneath, but a dark translucent overlay with loading text makes it feel broken. The shimmer skeletons elsewhere are well-done; this text overlay pattern is the outlier.

**Recommended fix:** Show shimmer placeholder where the score would be. Don't overlay text on the photo. The photo should always look clean and inviting.

### 3. Hero Card "WAIT" Badge on First Load

The hero card ("Your Best Window Right Now") showed a red "WAIT" badge for Grand Targhee Resort on load. The hero is supposed to be GO-only per the CLAUDE.md decisions. If no GO venue exists, the hero should either show nothing or show a loading state — never a WAIT badge. A red warning on the very first thing users see is anti-conversion.

**Note:** This may be a timing issue — conditions still loading when hero renders. But the result is the same: user's first impression is a red "WAIT" label.

### 3. Email Capture Goes Nowhere (P1 — Line 7214)

Still `alert("You're on the list! 🎉")`. Every email entered is permanently lost. With ProductHunt 7 days away, this is the highest-ROI fix — captured emails become upvoters, beta testers, and early adopters.

Options (in order of speed):

3. **Mailchimp embedded form** — best for long-term, needs signup.

## Inspiration

**Surfline's session detail page.** When you tap a surf spot on Surfline, the detail view opens with a full-bleed photo, a clean white card below with conditions data, and a bold "Check Surf" CTA button. No emoji. No vote buttons. No clutter. The data speaks — wave height, wind, swell period, tide — presented in a tight grid with consistent typography. The booking/camera CTA is one button, not two competing CTAs side by side.

Peakly's VenueDetailSheet is 80% there but loses to Surfline on information density and visual cleanliness. Surfline packs more useful data into less space with zero decorative elements.

---

## Decision Made

**"Checking conditions..." text overlay on card photos must die.** The shimmer skeleton pattern is already implemented and working well for text placeholders. The dark text overlay on photos is the visual equivalent of putting a "LOADING" sticker on a beautiful painting. Cards should always look inviting — show the photo clean, show a shimmer placeholder where the score badge will go, and let the score pop in when ready. This applies to CompactCard, ListingCard, and the hero card.
