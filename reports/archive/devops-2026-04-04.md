| Open-Meteo rate limit exposure | 3,726 venues × 2 calls (weather + marine) = 7,452 calls | ⚠️ Monitor |

At 10,000 daily active users opening the app, batched weather fetching and the 30-min localStorage cache prevent rate limit exhaustion. The cache means the same venue's weather is fetched at most 48×/day per device, not per user — acceptable at current scale.

**Concern at scale:** If 1,000 users open the app simultaneously with cold caches (e.g., after a deploy that busts the cache), the batched fetcher would fire 1,000 × 75 batches of Open-Meteo requests. At 10K calls/day free tier, this means ~13 simultaneous cold-cache users could exhaust the daily quota. Consider: a shared server-side weather cache (even a daily cron that pre-fetches and stores results) would eliminate this risk entirely.

## 7. What Breaks First at Scale

**The Open-Meteo rate limit is the first thing that fails at scale.** Here's the math: Open-Meteo free tier is 10,000 calls/day. At 3,726 venues needing 2 API calls each (weather + marine), a full cold cache load = 7,452 calls. With the 30-minute TTL and per-device caching, a single device triggers at most 48 full refreshes/day × 7,452 = 357,696 calls. But each device only fetches what it needs (paginated, 30/page, batched). In practice, 2–5 concurrent cold-cache users can exhaust the daily free quota.

**How to prevent it:** Add a lightweight server-side weather proxy that caches Open-Meteo responses for 30 minutes and serves all clients from a single source. This can run on the existing VPS. Estimated dev time: 2 hours. Deploy this before 1K MAU.
