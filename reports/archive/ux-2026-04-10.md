## Top 3 Visual Issues

### 1. The emoji plague (Day 16)

Confirmed live in code today via grep. Exact locations:

- `app.jsx:6879` — `{c.emoji} {c.label}` in category pills
- `app.jsx:8316` — `"Share Peakly · Invite Friends 🎁"` in Profile share button
- `app.jsx:9571` — `>💡 You'd also like</div>` in VenueDetailSheet
- `app.jsx:9598` — `>🎯 Insider Tips</div>` in VenueDetailSheet
- `app.jsx:9637` — `>🎟️ Book an experience</div>` in VenueDetailSheet

Go look at the Airbnb homepage, AllTrails Explore, KAYAK results, Hopper, Booking.com. Count the emoji in their UI chrome. The number is zero. Peakly's category pills — the first thing a user sees after onboarding — still look like a Discord server. **This alone puts Peakly in the "hobby app" bucket regardless of every other polish we've done.**

### 2. Hero photo overlay still at 0.58 opacity

VenueDetailSheet hero photo gets absolutely crushed by `rgba(0,0,0,0.58)`. On dark photos (any night/dusk shot, any dark-water surf break, most ski venues) the hero becomes a near-black rectangle with unreadable text. Airbnb uses a 0–0.35 gradient. Peakly's venue title already has `textShadow` for legibility — the scrim is overbuilt insurance that destroys the photo itself.

### 3. "Checking conditions..." shimmer dominates Explore grid

With 3,726 venues batch-fetching at 50/2s, the first 30–60 seconds of an Explore session show shimmer placeholders instead of content. The card photos, names, and flight prices should render immediately; only the score badge should wait. Right now the loading badge overlays the photo and the whole grid looks broken. This is a conversion killer on first visit — the exact moment where we need to earn trust.

### 1. Score vote buttons still wired up (lines 9305, 9314–9323, 9510–9515)

```
9305:  const [scoreVotes, setScoreVotes] = useLocalStorage("peakly_score_votes", {});
```

Users do not understand what voting "up" or "down" on a wave-height score means. The buttons add visual noise to an already-dense conditions panel. This was cut 16 days ago. It's still here. **This is the single cleanest, most surgical delete on the board** — 3 lines of state, 10 lines of handler, 8 lines of JSX.

### 2. No loading skeleton for hero card

The "YOUR BEST WINDOW RIGHT NOW" hero renders with whatever partial data exists before weather finishes fetching. Users see Grand Targhee with a WAIT badge and a score of 50 — which is the worst possible first impression ("the app's top recommendation is a WAIT??"). Needs a full-width shimmer until at least 1 GO-scored venue is available.

### 3. Alerts tab empty state is 60% white space

High-conversion surface wasted. Airbnb's saved-search empty state shows what you'd get. Peakly's just says "trust us." Mock 2–3 example alert cards (grayed out) showing "Pipeline hits epic — flight drops below $400." That's the whole value prop in one visual.

## Inspiration — Steal from Hopper

Hopper's "price prediction" pill on flight results is the clearest, calmest UI element in travel booking: a single-line label that says "Buy now — prices expected to rise" or "Wait — 80% chance of drop." No emoji. No color explosion. Just a decisive recommendation with confidence level.

Peakly's score pills currently show the number (`78`) and a label (`GO` / `WAIT`). That's half of it. The winning version is a single-sentence recommendation:

> **GO this weekend — conditions peak Sat, flights $289 (↓ last 7d)**

That one line collapses the entire value prop into something a user can quote to a friend. That's The Window Score vision from CLAUDE.md made concrete. Ship a v0 of that sentence line on ListingCard before ProductHunt and it becomes the thing that gets screenshot and shared.
