# Competitor Watch Report

**Date:** March 24, 2026
**Agent:** Competitor Watch (Growth Lead)

---

## Per-Competitor Summary

### 1. Surfline

**What they do:** Surf forecasts, HD live cams, session tracking.

**Recent moves:**
- **Price hike (April 2025):** Premium jumped 21% from $99 to $119.99/year. New ad-supported tier at $69.99/year. Premium+ at $149.99/year (shareable with 2 accounts, ~$50/user).
- **Session matching (Oct 2025):** New feature shows when upcoming forecasts match conditions from your favorite past sessions -- helping surfers spot days similar to their best ones.
- **Social sharing (March 2025):** Users can now send live cam links, Cam Rewind footage, and spot guides directly to friends.
- **iOS 26 compatibility fix (Sept 2025).**
- Update cadence slowed to roughly every two months in 2026.

**Peakly relevance:** Surfline remains single-sport with zero flight integration. Their $120/year price point makes Peakly Pro at $79/year a clear value play for multi-sport users. Their session-matching feature is clever -- Peakly could build something similar using the scoring history concept.

---

### 2. OnTheSnow

**What they do:** Ski conditions, snow reports, webcams for 2,000+ resorts.

**Recent moves:**
- Steady maintenance updates (latest Android: v10.4.21, Dec 2025).
- Core features unchanged: powder alerts, resort comparison, user-submitted photos/reports.
- No major new feature launches detected for 2025-2026 season.
- Published a "Best ski apps for 2025-26" article positioning themselves as the hub.

**Peakly relevance:** OnTheSnow is coasting on their existing user base without meaningful innovation. No flights, no trip planning, no AI features. This is Peakly's easiest competitor to leapfrog -- their ski users get zero help with booking travel.

---

### 3. AllTrails

**What they do:** Hiking/biking trails, maps, navigation. 5M+ downloads.

**Recent moves:**
- **AllTrails Peak tier launched (May 2025):** $80/year -- AI-powered premium tier.
- **AI Custom Routes:** Create routes from scratch or modify existing ones (shorter, less steep, more scenic). Desktop version went free for all members (Nov 2025).
- **Trail Conditions:** Real-time weather, precipitation, snow depth, and mosquito activity on every trail. Was Peak-only, moved to Plus tier in Nov 2025.
- **Community Heatmaps:** Shows trail crowdedness -- avoid or seek out popular spots.
- **Outdoor Lens:** Camera-based plant/tree identification (AI-powered).
- **Points of Interest:** 500K+ mapped viewpoints, landmarks, trail amenities added across all tiers.
- Trail Conditions feature moving down from Peak to Plus signals they are using conditions as a broader retention tool, not a premium differentiator.

**Peakly relevance:** AllTrails is the most aggressive competitor in terms of AI feature velocity. Their $80/year Peak tier is nearly identical pricing to Peakly Pro ($79/year). However, they remain hiking/biking focused with ZERO flight integration. Their "Trail Conditions" feature validates that conditions-aware travel planning has mainstream demand. Peakly should watch their crowd heatmap feature closely -- it maps directly to the Phase 7 Crowd Intelligence vision.

---

### 4. FATMAP / Strava

**What they do:** Activity tracking, social fitness, 3D maps (post-FATMAP acquisition).

**Recent moves:**
- **FATMAP fully shut down (Fall 2024):** Standalone app is dead. All tech migrated to Strava.
- **Proprietary Map Rendering Engine (March 2025):** Strava launched its own mapping engine powered by FATMAP tech -- lifelike 3D terrain, detailed satellite imagery.
- **Winter map layers:** Avalanche gradient (25-45+ degree slope risk) and slope angle overlays.
- **Runna acquisition (April 2025):** Strava acquired AI running coach app Runna. Bundled at $149.99/year.
- **Athlete Intelligence:** AI workout summaries.
- **Pricing:** $79.99/year or $11.99/month. Family plan at $139.99/year.

**Peakly relevance:** Strava is becoming a fitness super-app but remains activity-tracking focused, not trip-planning focused. Their avalanche/slope data is relevant to backcountry skiers but does not help users decide WHEN or WHERE to go. No flight integration whatsoever. Strava's $80/year price point is another data point validating Peakly Pro at $79/year.

---

### 5. KAYAK

**What they do:** Meta-search for flights, hotels, cars. 50M+ users.

**Recent moves:**
- **2026 Travel Trends Report:** Highlights "soft adventure" as a major trend -- 25% of travelers now mix light adventure with relaxation. 23% self-identify as adventure/nature seekers.
- **AI trust growing:** 41% of surveyed travelers would trust AI recommendations over friends/social media.
- **Sleepcations filter:** New Explore filter for wellness-focused destinations.
- **Buy Now, Pay Later filter:** Integrated payment flexibility.
- **Premium economy up 18% YoY, luxury hotels up 128% YoY.**

**Peakly relevance:** KAYAK is identifying the adventure travel segment as a growth trend but is NOT building adventure-specific tools. They remain a generic flight/hotel search engine. Their own data shows 25% of travelers want adventure + relaxation combos -- this is Peakly's exact target user. KAYAK's AI trust stat (41%) validates Peakly's vibe search and AI trip builder direction.

---

### 6. Hopper

**What they do:** AI-powered flight price prediction, flexible booking.

**Recent moves:**
- **$850M revenue in 2024.** 40% from fintech products (Price Freeze, cancellation insurance).
- **35M annual users (2024).**
- **Annual bookings: $7.5B.**
- Core features stable: 95% price prediction accuracy, Price Freeze, flight disruption guarantee, rental cars.
- No adventure-specific features detected.

**Peakly relevance:** Hopper proves the "when to book" model works at massive scale. Their Price Freeze concept could inspire a Peakly feature ("Freeze this window" -- lock in a flight price when conditions look good). Hopper remains completely conditions-blind and sport-agnostic.

---

### 7. NEW COMPETITOR: SurfTrips.ai

**What they do:** AI-powered surf trip planner combining conditions + flights + accommodation.

**Recent moves:**
- Appears to be a newer entrant (likely 2024-2025 launch).
- Enter your airport and dates, get surf spot recommendations ranked by flight time.
- Real-time flight and accommodation pricing integrated.
- Filtering by surf style and vibe.
- Community-sourced spot feedback.

**Peakly relevance:** THIS IS THE CLOSEST DIRECT COMPETITOR. SurfTrips.ai combines conditions + flights for surf specifically -- the exact model Peakly uses across multiple sports. Key differences: SurfTrips.ai is surf-only, appears to be web-only, and lacks the real-time scoring/Window Score concept. Peakly's multi-sport advantage and proprietary scoring system are differentiators, but SurfTrips.ai validates the conditions+flights model and could expand to other sports.

## 3 Opportunities to Exploit

### 1. Surfline's Price Hike Creates a Window
Surfline raised prices 21% to $120/year and introduced an ad-supported tier. Their community is vocal about pricing frustration. Peakly Pro at $79/year with multi-sport coverage and flight integration is a compelling alternative for surfers who also ski, hike, or travel. **Action:** Target Surfline users on Reddit (r/surfing) with messaging around "Why pay $120 for just surf when you get surf + ski + flights for $79?"

### 2. AllTrails Validated Conditions-Aware Travel at Scale
AllTrails adding Trail Conditions to their Plus tier (not just Peak) signals that conditions data is becoming table-stakes for outdoor apps. Their 5M+ user base is now trained to expect weather/conditions in trip planning. When those users want to plan a surf or ski trip, AllTrails cannot help them. **Action:** Position Peakly as "AllTrails for adventure travel" in content marketing. Build SEO content targeting "best surf conditions this week" and "best ski conditions + cheap flights" queries.

### 3. KAYAK's Own Data Shows 25% of Travelers Want Adventure
KAYAK identified adventure-seekers as a quarter of all travelers but is not building tools for them. Their AI trust stat (41%) also validates Peakly's vibe search direction. **Action:** Build an "adventure mode" landing page optimized for the search terms KAYAK identified. Consider a KAYAK-to-Peakly bridge: "Found a cheap flight on KAYAK? Check conditions on Peakly."

## 3 Threats to Watch

### 1. SurfTrips.ai -- Direct Model Overlap
SurfTrips.ai is doing conditions + flights for surf trips. If they expand to ski and other sports, they become a direct Peakly competitor. They appear to have real-time pricing and community features. **Mitigation:** Accelerate the Window Score (Phase 2 roadmap) -- a proprietary combined metric is harder to replicate than just showing conditions alongside flights. Ship it before SurfTrips.ai expands.

### 2. AllTrails Expanding Into Conditions Intelligence
AllTrails moved Trail Conditions from premium to mid-tier, suggesting they see it as core infrastructure, not a luxury feature. With 5M+ downloads, massive resources, and proven AI capability (Custom Routes, Outdoor Lens), AllTrails could expand into surf/ski conditions if they decide adventure travel is their next market. **Mitigation:** Peakly's flight integration is the moat AllTrails cannot easily replicate. Double down on the conditions + flights combination.

### 3. AI Trip Planners Commoditizing the Space
Generic AI trip planners (Layla, Wanderlog, etc.) are getting smarter and could add conditions data to their planning flows. If ChatGPT or Google adds "check surf conditions" to their travel planning, the barrier drops significantly. **Mitigation:** Build proprietary data assets (Window Score history, user preference patterns, crowd intelligence) that generic AI cannot easily replicate. Speed matters -- establish Peakly as the category name before AI planners catch up.

## Key Takeaway

Peakly's multi-sport conditions + flights combination remains unique. No major player has replicated it. However, SurfTrips.ai has validated the conditions+flights model in the surf vertical, and AllTrails is aggressively building conditions intelligence. The window is open but narrowing. The Window Score (Phase 2) is the single most important differentiator to ship -- it creates a proprietary metric that is genuinely hard to copy.
