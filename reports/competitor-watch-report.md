## 1. Direct Competitors — Latest Updates

### Surfline
**Pricing:** Premium $119.99/yr | Premium w/Ads $69.99/yr | Premium+ $149.99/yr (shareable, 3 accounts)

**Recent moves:**
- **Premium+ AI features now live:** Wave Timeline, Wave Distribution, Crowd Prediction, and Smart Clips powered by computer vision. Available in US and Australia, expanding globally. This is Surfline's biggest product investment in years.
- **High-res nearshore model:** New coastal swell detail in charts using detailed bathymetry and local wave physics. Meaningful forecast accuracy improvement.
- **Sessions expanding on Android:** Strava/Garmin import, Apple Watch session logging with complication for single-tap logging. Sessions now free for all users.
- **Surf Windows:** Time-aware alerting that respects user schedules -- only notifies when conditions are good during hours you can actually surf.
- **Swell rose brought back** to spot page; shows wind + swell interaction in one view.
- **Favorites moving to bottom nav** on mobile (iOS rollout week of June 23).

**User sentiment (Trustpilot, App Store, Reddit):**
- Forecast accuracy remains the #1 complaint: "They already get 99% of their forecasts wrong and now an upcharge?"
- Free tier now limited to 5 surf checks/week -- aggressive paywall pushing users away
- Excessive ads in free tier (15-second ads for 20 seconds of video)
- Camera outages reported frequently
- Poor iPad support, no landscape mode
- Duplicate charges and unresponsive customer service reported

**Gap Peakly owns:** Single sport. No flights. No trip planning. No "when should I go?" No multi-sport. Surfline tells you what conditions ARE -- Peakly tells you when conditions WILL BE optimal combined with cheap flights.

### AllTrails
**Pricing:** Plus $35.99/yr | Peak $80/yr (aggressive $18/yr promo ran in Jan 2026)

**Recent moves:**
- **AllTrails Peak launched ($80/yr):** AI-powered custom routes, trail conditions with weather data overlay, community heatmaps, Outdoor Lens (plant/insect identification via camera).
- **ChatGPT integration (Nov 2025):** AllTrails routes now surface directly inside ChatGPT. Significant distribution play -- meeting users in the AI assistant layer.
- **Trail Conditions expanded:** Now includes ground conditions (mud, snow, ice), weather, daylight, bug activity, snowpack at Plus tier.
- **Apple Watch expansion:** Compass, real-time stats, heart rate, distance remaining, wrong-turn alerts.
- **500,000 mapped viewpoints/amenities** added for all users.
- **CTO interview (Jan 2026):** Discussed "the algorithmic outdoors" -- heavy AI investment continuing.

**User complaints:** Minimal -- AllTrails has strong user satisfaction. The Peak tier at $80/yr is the main friction point (some users feel it should be included in Plus).

**Gap Peakly owns:** Domestic/hiking focus. No real-time scoring of "is today a good day?" No flight integration. No surf/ski conditions. No cross-sport intelligence.

**Recent moves:** Effectively stagnant. Last major update December 2025.

### OpenSnow
**Pricing:** Base $49.99/yr (1 person) | $89.99/yr (4 people)

**Recent moves:**
- **PEAKS model (2025-2026 season):** New in-house forecast model claiming up to 50% more accuracy in mountain terrain. Learns from past storms. Serious technical investment.
- **AI Overviews:** AI-written plain-English condition summaries for popular resorts worldwide.
- **Premium features paywalled** as of Nov 2025 -- free preview removed. 11-15 day forecasts, multi-model comparison, global storm maps moved to Premium only.
- **Pricing backlash significant:** Users complaining $50/yr is "far too high for a single recreational skier." Single-user plan was discontinued (minimum is now the $50 plan).

**Gap Peakly owns:** Ski only. Zero flight integration. Zero trip planning. No multi-sport. Pricing backlash creates an opening for Peakly's free tier.

## 2. Adjacent Threats

### SweetSpot (findsweetspot.app) -- CLOSEST OVERLAP
Cross-references live weather forecasts with real-time flight prices to discover destinations "when it's best and cheapest to go." This is essentially Peakly's tagline.

**Assessment:** Early stage, general-purpose weather + flights without sport-specific scoring. Peakly's advantage is adventure-sport specificity (wave height, snow depth, UV index vs. generic "good weather"). But SweetSpot validates the market and could expand. **Monitor closely.**

### SurfTrips.ai -- DIRECT SURF COMPETITOR
AI-powered surf trip planner combining conditions + flights + accommodation. Active blog content building SEO authority. Destinations include Portugal, Mexico, Costa Rica, Hawaii, Puerto Rico, France, Nicaragua, El Salvador, Australia.

**Assessment:** Still web-only, still surf-only. No multi-sport expansion detected. But they own the "surf + flights" keyword space. Peakly should be building equivalent SEO content across all sports.

### Gate to Surf (gatetosurf.com)
Surf-specific trip planning: spot ratings by difficulty, verified schools, board rentals, forecasts "in plain English." Claims 20-minute trip planning.

**Assessment:** Accommodation/school marketplace, not conditions + flights intelligence. Not a direct threat but competes for same surfer audience.

### Wavecation
Surf accommodation marketplace mapping thousands of breaks with insider tips, crowd levels, surf-friendly stays.

**Assessment:** Accommodation-centric. No flight pricing. No live scoring. Potential affiliate partner rather than competitor.

### Lonely Planet App (launched March 2026)
Expert destination guides, maps, day-by-day itineraries. AI assistant coming (trained on decades of LP content). 450+ on-the-ground experts. Free year for early adopters.

**Assessment:** Traditional editorial travel -- no conditions, no forecasts, no flights. Brand authority is the threat, not product overlap. Watch for adventure/conditions feature additions.

### Thermal
Marketplace for guided adventure trips (surf, ski, cycling). Multi-sport positioning. $1K-$5K guided experiences.

**Assessment:** Adjacent, not competitive. Sells guided experiences, not self-service planning. Potential affiliate partner.

### No direct "conditions + flights + multi-sport" competitor found.
PhocusWire Hot 25 for 2026 (150+ startups reviewed) contains zero apps combining live conditions with flight pricing for adventure sports. **Peakly's positioning remains unique.**

### Current Landscape

| Surfline Premium | $119.99 | Surf only |

| **Peakly Pro** | **$79.00** | **Multi-sport + flights** |
| Surfline Premium w/Ads | $69.99 | Surf only + ads |
| Windy.app Pro | ~$59.99 | Weather only |
| OpenSnow Base | $49.99 | Ski only (1 user) |
| AllTrails Plus | $35.99 | Hiking/trails basic |

### Is $79/yr correct?

**Yes. $79/yr is optimally positioned.**

1. **Anchored against AllTrails Peak ($80/yr)** -- nearly identical price for a premium outdoor app. Users accept this price point.
2. **34% cheaper than Surfline Premium ($120/yr)** while offering more breadth (multi-sport + flights vs. single-sport).
3. **Above OpenSnow ($50/yr)** -- justified because Peakly covers multiple sports + includes flight pricing.
4. **Annual billing converts better** -- $79/yr is psychologically cheaper than equivalent $9/month ($108/yr effective).
5. **Not so cheap it signals hobby project** ($29 would undermine perceived quality), not so expensive it triggers comparison shopping ($120+).

**Recommendation:** Hold at $79/yr. Consider a launch promotion at $49/yr for first year to build subscriber base, then grandfather early adopters.

1. **Surfline's AI Crowd Prediction** -- Build crowd estimates into Peakly's scoring (Phase 7 roadmap). High-value feature users care about.
2. **Surfline's Surf Windows (schedule-aware alerting)** -- "Only alert me when conditions are good AND I'm free AND flights are cheap." No competitor does the three-variable version.
3. **AllTrails' ChatGPT integration** -- Get Peakly's conditions+flights data into the AI assistant layer (ChatGPT, Gemini). 70% of travelers use AI for planning. This is a distribution imperative.
4. **OpenSnow's AI Overviews** -- Natural-language condition summaries on venue cards ("Surf is firing -- 6ft swell, offshore winds, flights from LAX $189 RT").
5. **Hopper's fintech model** -- 40% of revenue from Price Freeze/insurance. Peakly Pro could offer a "price lock" feature for flights.

## 7. Gaps Peakly Can Own

1. **"Conditions + flights" in one view** -- Nobody does this at multi-sport scale. SweetSpot is closest but lacks sport-specific scoring. SurfTrips.ai does it for surf only.
2. **Multi-sport adventure platform** -- Every competitor is single-sport. Peakly is the only cross-sport conditions + flights platform.
3. **The Window Score** -- No competitor has a single combined metric for conditions + price + timing. This is the moat.
4. **Price-sensitive adventure travelers** -- Surfline at $120/yr and OpenSnow at $50/yr generating backlash. Peakly's free tier captures defectors.
5. **The "juggling tabs" problem** -- Conditions, flights, weather, hotels across 4+ apps. Peakly consolidates.

## 8. One Thing That Should Change Peakly's Roadmap

**KAYAK's AI Mode + AllTrails' ChatGPT integration signal that AI-assisted trip planning is now mainstream, not emerging.** 55% of Gen Z would change their destination based on AI suggestion. AllTrails is already inside ChatGPT. KAYAK now offers vibe-based discovery at 50M user scale.

**What this means for Peakly:** The Window Score (Phase 2) is no longer just a "nice to have" -- it's the moat that prevents KAYAK from eating Peakly's lunch when they inevitably add a conditions layer. And a ChatGPT/AI assistant integration is now a distribution imperative, not a future nice-to-have. If someone asks ChatGPT "where should I surf next week?" and Peakly isn't in the answer, that user is lost.

**Recommended priority shift:**
1. Ship Window Score in next 60 days (before KAYAK adds conditions)
2. Build ChatGPT plugin/OpenAI action (before more competitors embed in AI assistants)
3. Then resume the pre-launch checklist (PWA, analytics, onboarding)
