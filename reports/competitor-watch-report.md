## Competitor Moves Worth Copying

1. **Surfline Premium+ AI features ($149.99/yr):** Smart Cams at 160+ spots using AI for wave timeline, wave distribution, crowd predictions, and smart clips. The AI angle is compelling — Peakly's Vibe Search is a differentiator but could be marketed harder as "AI-powered."

2. **AllTrails AI Smart Routing:** Automatically reroutes if trail closed or storm incoming. Analogous concept for Peakly: auto-suggest alternative venues when a user's target spot has bad conditions.

3. **AllTrails Community Heatmaps (crowd data):** Uses anonymous telemetry to show which trails are crowded vs empty. This is Phase 7 of Peakly's roadmap (Crowd Intelligence). AllTrails shipped it first — validates the concept.

4. **OpenSnow PEAKS model (proprietary forecasting):** 50% more accurate in mountain terrain, 15-day forecasts, multi-model comparison. OpenSnow posted a banner season with 40% business growth despite brutal conditions. Lesson: proprietary forecasting models are worth the investment.

5. **Hopper Price Freeze:** Hold fares for up to 7 days before booking. Peakly could integrate a similar concept — "Lock this deal while you check conditions."

## Gaps Peakly Can Own

1. **Multi-sport + flights in one app.** No competitor combines real-time conditions across surfing, skiing, AND beach with flight pricing. Surfline is surf-only. OpenSnow is ski-only. AllTrails has no flights. KAYAK has no conditions. This remains Peakly's primary moat.

2. **Conditions-triggered flight alerts.** Nobody sends "conditions just went epic AND flights are cheap" notifications. Surfline alerts on conditions. Hopper alerts on prices. Peakly can alert on BOTH simultaneously — this is the Strike Mission concept and nobody else is building it.

3. **Budget adventure travel planning.** Surfer magazine highlighted top budget surf trips for 2026 — the demand for budget-conscious adventure travel is high. Peakly's deal-based flight scoring already addresses this. Market it harder.

4. **Cross-sport trip optimization.** SKI Magazine featured destinations where you can ski and surf in the same day. Peakly's multi-sport data could power a "ski + surf combo trip" feature that no single-sport app can touch.

5. **OnTheSnow user frustration.** Recent reviews flag the app as "buggy and unusable" after major changes. Their users are looking for alternatives. OpenSnow is eating their lunch on the premium end ($49-100/yr). Peakly could capture the casual skier who just wants conditions + flights.

1. **Multi-app workflow frustration.** Surfers and skiers routinely describe checking Surfline/OpenSnow for conditions, then separately checking Google Flights/Hopper for prices, then trying to line everything up manually. Peakly eliminates 3 separate apps.

## Adjacent Threats to Monitor

1. **Da Surf Engine** — Surf trip planning app letting users filter by wave type, water temp, and month. Niche but overlaps with Peakly's surf venue discovery. Not a major threat yet.

2. **WeSki / Heidi** — Ski trip booking platforms combining conditions with accommodation packages. More OTA than conditions app, but moving into Peakly's territory.

3. **Dream Trip (AI itinerary planner)** — Lets users save places from TikTok/Instagram and builds AI itineraries. Not conditions-aware, but the AI trip planning UX is slick. Watch for them adding weather/conditions data.

4. **FutureFlights.ai** — New entrant specifically covering "skiing flights" with smart routing. Very early but targeting the exact conditions + flights intersection.

**No competitor currently combines multi-sport conditions + flight pricing + AI matching.** Peakly's positioning remains unique. The window is still open.
