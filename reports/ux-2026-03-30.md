# UX Designer Report — 2026-03-30

**Design Score: 7.2/10**

Peakly has come a long way. The splash screen is premium, the card system is cohesive, and the VenueDetailSheet is well-structured with a clear conversion funnel. But with Reddit soft launch tomorrow, there are a few things that will hurt first impressions with a critical, design-savvy audience.

---

## Top 3 Visual Issues

### 1. Emoji pollution in section headers (Medium severity)
Despite the CLAUDE.md decision to "remove emoji from UI chrome," emoji still litters section headers throughout the app:
- VenueDetailSheet: "💡 You'd also like", "🎯 Insider Tips", "🛍️ Gear for this trip", "🎟️ Book an experience", "📂 Save to list", "📤 Share this trip"
- Category pills still use emoji prefixes: `{c.emoji} {c.label}` (line 6844)
- Saved venues header uses "❤️" in the pill
- Alert creation: "✨ Any sport"

This clashes with the premium, Airbnb/KAYAK-level aesthetic. Airbnb uses zero emoji in headers. Neither does Surfline.

**Component/lines:** ExploreTab (6844), VenueDetailSheet (9540, 9567, 9582, 9606, 9671), AlertsTab (7428)

### 2. ListingCard "Book" button uses blue gradient instead of #222 CTA color (Low-Medium severity)
The CLAUDE.md design decision says CTA buttons use `#222` (dark, not blue). But the ListingCard and FeaturedCard "Book" buttons still use `background:"linear-gradient(135deg,#1a56db,#0ea5e9)"` (lines 5818, 5898). This creates visual inconsistency with the VenueDetailSheet sticky CTA bar which correctly uses `#222`.

**Component/lines:** ListingCard (5817-5822), FeaturedCard (5898)

### 3. Three-column "All" grid is too cramped on mobile (Medium severity)
When viewing "All" categories, the grid uses `gridTemplateColumns: "1fr 1fr 1fr"` with only 10px gap and 14px padding (line 7123-7127). On a 375px phone, each CompactCard gets ~107px width. The 128px image height with 10px font titles creates a cramped, thumbnail-like feel that's far from best-in-class. Compare to Airbnb which never goes below 2 columns for cards with meaningful content.

**Component/lines:** ExploreTab grid (7123-7128)

## Top 3 Interaction Issues

### 1. No loading feedback on venue tap → detail sheet (High severity)
When a user taps a card, `openDetail` fires (line 10776) which sets the venue and starts a weather fetch. But between the tap and the sheet animation, there's no intermediate loading state. On slow connections with 3,726 venues, the weather fetch can take a beat. The sheet opens with "Checking conditions…" which feels broken.

**Fix:** Add a brief skeleton/shimmer state or ensure the sheet pre-loads weather data from cache before animating in.

**Component/lines:** App.openDetail (10776-10783), VenueDetailSheet

### 2. BottomNav touch targets are undersized (Medium severity)
The BottomNav buttons have `padding:"8px 0"` (line 10364) with no explicit width. The SVG icons are 20x20. Combined with the label (10px font), the total touch area is roughly 20x40px — below the 44x44px minimum recommended by Apple HIG and WCAG. On a phone, users will mis-tap between Explore/Alerts/Profile.

**Component/lines:** BottomNav (10360-10378)

### 3. Share button in VenueDetailSheet hero overlaps with Save button on narrow screens (Low severity)
The hero header has "📤 Share & Invite" and "🤍 Save" buttons side by side at `top:24, right:12` (line 9427-9430). Both have `padding:"6px 13px"` and `fontSize:12`. On a 320px-wide device, these buttons crowd together. The text-heavy labels ("Share & Invite" is 14 characters) could overflow or touch.

**Component/lines:** VenueDetailSheet hero buttons (9427-9430)

## Inspiration

**Steal from Surfline:** Their forecast cards use a clean horizontal date strip with a subtle gradient background that highlights "today" vs future days. Peakly's 7-day forecast in VenueDetailSheet (line 9507-9522) is functional but lacks visual hierarchy. Surfline makes the current day's card 20% wider with a soft blue highlight and larger weather icon, which draws the eye naturally. This single change would make the forecast section feel 2x more polished.

---

## Decision Made

**Emoji removal from all section headers ships today.** Headers will use clean text only (e.g., "You'd also like" not "💡 You'd also like"). Emoji stays acceptable in: category pill labels (functional taxonomy), inline badges/tags (data), and notification/status content. But all `<div>` section headers in VenueDetailSheet, ExploreTab, and AlertsTab lose their emoji prefixes. This aligns with the March 23 design decision and brings us closer to Airbnb-level restraint.

## Pre-Reddit Launch Polish Notes

Tomorrow is the Reddit soft launch. Three quick wins that would meaningfully improve first impressions:

1. **Emoji cleanup** (described above) — 15 min fix, huge perception improvement
2. **Book button color standardization** — 2 min fix, visual consistency
3. **BottomNav padding** — 2 min fix, reduces mis-taps that frustrate new users

The 3-column grid change is slightly more risky (could affect scroll behavior with pagination) — would recommend shipping that post-launch with testing.
