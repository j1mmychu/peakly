## Issues Found

### P1 — Duplicate Titles With Conflicting Data (LAUNCH BLOCKER)

**242 duplicate titles covering 507 venue instances.** 34 of those duplicates span more than one category, and several repeat the same dangerous generic tag set. Examples pulled straight from the array:

- **Teahupo'o** — 5 entries total. `teahupoo-s141` is tagged `Beach Break,All Levels,Consistent Swell,Longboard Friendly`. Teahupo'o is a shallow-reef double-up that broke a pro surfer's back last month. Calling it a beach break is a legal problem.
- **Sayulita** — 3 entries, one surfing, one tanning, one surfing again (`sayulita-s76` generic "Beach Break, All Levels").
- **Fernando de Noronha** — 3 entries split across surfing/tanning/surfing; `fernando-de-noronha-s20` tagged "Expert Level" while `noronha_surf` says "Crystal Waters" — same place, contradictory framing.
- **Diani Beach** — 5 entries across tanning/surfing/kite/kite/tanning. Two of the five are kite duplicates.
- **Clearwater Beach** — 4 entries, 2 kite + 2 tanning, same airport, same city.
- **Mui Ne** — 4 entries (1 surf + 3 kite). Three kite duplicates in one place.
- **Jericoacoara, Raja Ampat, Railay Beach** — each 3–4 entries across multiple categories.

**Recommendation:** Dedup before ProductHunt. The safe merge pattern is: keep the earliest-id entry, move any unique tags onto it, and delete the rest. I've attached a dedup worksheet at `reports/venue-dedup-worksheet-2026-04-10.md` below.

Generic "Beach Break, All Levels, Consistent Swell, Longboard Friendly" tag set appears on at least 12 expansion venues that are absolutely not beach breaks. This is the templated-expansion fingerprint — someone generated 500 surf venues with a copy-paste tag palette. Needs a pass specifically for expert-only spots.

### P1 — 686 Airport Codes Missing From AIRPORT_CITY

Out of 813 unique airport codes in `VENUES`, only 127 (16%) have a human-readable city label. The other 2,407 venues render the raw IATA code in the card header and search bar.

Sample of missing: `CNS` (Cairns), `AGP` (Málaga), `GVA` (Geneva), `ZQN` (Queenstown), `ZRH` (Zurich), `EGE` (Vail/Eagle), `MTJ` (Telluride/Montrose), `HDN` (Steamboat/Hayden), `SUN` (Sun Valley), `INN` (Innsbruck), `SZG` (Salzburg), `VCE` (Venice), `CTS` (Sapporo), `CBR` (Canberra), `CMF` (Chambéry), `GCM` (Grand Cayman), `PLS` (Turks & Caicos), `AUA` (Aruba).

These are NOT obscure airports. Vail, Queenstown, Sun Valley, Zurich, Innsbruck, Aruba — these are exactly the destinations the hero card is supposed to make look aspirational. They currently render as "EGE, United States". Embarrassing.

**Fix:** bulk-add 100 well-known airports to `AIRPORT_CITY` (app.jsx:5474). I can generate the patch in 15 minutes of focused work — this is a data entry task, not a design decision.

### P1 — 614 Airport Codes Missing From AP_CONTINENT

1,623 venues (43.6%) fall through to the `"europe"` default in the pricing fallback (app.jsx:5415). That produces systematically wrong flight estimates — a Hakuba trip from JFK gets priced as a European flight.

**Fix:** bulk-add to `AP_CONTINENT` (app.jsx:192). Same patch as the AIRPORT_CITY work, different column.

### P1 — 739 Destination Airports Missing From BASE_PRICES

`BASE_PRICES` only has 76 destination entries. 737 destinations fall back to the region-route table. That table is OK for ballpark estimates but gets embarrassing for long-tail destinations — a Cayman Islands flight out of Denver comes back at $650 whether it's summer or January.

Not a launch blocker (the fallback works) but worth noting: the CLAUDE.md claim that region fallback "covers all 776 airports" is technically true but it's a single region-average, not per-airport. If Jack wants the app to feel precise, BASE_PRICES needs to be expanded to at least the top 100 most-used destinations.

### P1 — Photo Duplication (Updated Numbers)

CLAUDE.md says "93% photo reuse across 3,726 venues (only 257 unique images)." The current state is better than that but still not great:

- **Unique photos:** 2,261 out of 3,726 (60.7%)
- **Distinct photos reused across multiple venues:** 142
- **Most-reused photo:** appears 17 times

Still a P1 for launch polish — visitors who scroll past 30 ski venues shouldn't see the same snowy lift photo four times. I recommend targeting >90% uniqueness before ProductHunt, which means replacing ~1,400 photos. That's ~2 hours of scripted work against Unsplash.

### P2 — Tag Quality

Expansion venues carry template tag sets like `Beach Break | All Levels | Consistent Swell | Longboard Friendly` or `Reef Break | Offshore Winds | Expert Level | Barrel Waves`. They're not wrong often enough to be a P1, but they're not specific enough to feel trustworthy. Compare:

- Original: `Graveyard of the Atlantic | Swell Magnet` (Cape Hatteras) — specific, memorable
- Expansion: `Beach Break | All Levels | Consistent Swell` (also Cape Hatteras region) — generic

A prose pass over the expansion venues' tags is the single highest-leverage content improvement. Out of launch scope, but flag it for Phase 2.

## Content-Side Productivity Recommendations

Jack asked for self-improvement notes across Claude products. Three things that would make this agent run faster and cleaner:

1. **Move VENUES out of app.jsx into `data/venues.json`.** The file is 10,993 lines; 3,892 of them are one array. Every content edit right now forces a full reparse. A JSON split would make this agent's dedup patch a one-line `jq` pipe instead of a surgical Edit on a 1.5MB literal.
2. **Add `tasks/agents/data-sanity.sh`** that runs the audit checks I did here (dup titles, missing airport lookups, photo dupes) and exits non-zero on regression. Gate the GitHub Actions deploy on it. Prevents future expansion drops from re-breaking the same invariants.
3. **Lock the dedup worksheet as a CSV the Chief of Staff agent can read.** Daily cross-agent handoff is currently text-only — if the PM agent could `parse reports/venue-dedup-worksheet.csv` directly it would collapse the "agent X flagged it, agent Y tracked it, nothing shipped" cycle that CLAUDE.md itself flags as a six-day process failure.
