## 2. Critical Issue: Photo Duplication at 39.3%

The March 29 expansion (+500 surf, +500 ski, +500 beach) introduced severe photo duplication:

- **1,500 expansion venues share only 90 unique photos** between them.
- 60 photos are each reused 17 times. Another 30 photos are reused 16 times each.
- The original 2,226 venues have 2,171 unique photos (97.5% unique) -- the expansion batch is the sole problem.
- The three focus categories (Ski/Board, Surf, Beach) all have **67% photo duplication**, which is the worst in the dataset.
- Overall: 2,261 unique photos across 3,726 venues. 1,465 venues display a non-unique photo.

**Impact:** Users scrolling through the top 3 categories -- the exact categories being marketed on Reddit -- will see the same handful of images repeated 16-17 times each. This undermines the "every pixel matters" standard.

**Fix options:**
1. Source ~1,410 new Unsplash photo IDs for the expansion venues (best outcome, high effort).
2. Trim the expansion back to the 2,226 venues with unique photos (fast, loses venue breadth).
3. Use a photo rotation system that assigns from a larger pool at render time (medium effort, architectural change).
