## Competitive Landscape — Top 3 Threats

### 1. OpenSnow (Ski — Banner Season)
OpenSnow just posted a banner 2025-2026 season per BusinessDen (April 3). Their PEAKS AI model + custom powder alerts make them the gold standard for ski conditions. Pricing: $49.99/yr (Base) or $99.99/yr (Premium) for single users, with family plans up to $179.99/yr. They now offer 11-15 day forecasts, multi-model comparison ("Forecast Range"), and Super-Res Radar. **Key gap for Peakly to exploit:** OpenSnow is ski-only. No flights. No surf. No beach. No trip planning. They charge $100/yr for weather — Peakly gives conditions + flights for free across 9 sports.

### 2. Surfline (Surf — Still Dominant)
950+ live HD webcams, 16-day forecasts, daily expert reports. Massive install base. Surfline remains the default for "should I surf today?" but doesn't answer "where should I fly to surf this month?" That question — the travel-planning layer on top of conditions — is Peakly's entire thesis. Surfline would need to build flight integration from scratch to compete here.

### 3. Stormrider Surf Guide (Surf Travel — Direct Overlap)
Stormrider covers 5,000 surf spots with offline mapping, skill-level matching, seasonal filtering, and MagicSeaweed forecast integration. Their "Passport" subscription starts at ~$1.25/mo. This is the closest existing product to Peakly's surf travel positioning. **Critical difference:** Stormrider has no real-time scoring, no flight pricing, and no multi-sport coverage. But their 30-year content library and expert break-by-break descriptions are deeper than anything Peakly has. We should study their spot taxonomy.

### New Entrant: DropIn Surf
Social-first surf travel app — live location sharing, community events, trip planning. Different angle (community vs. conditions intelligence) but eating into the same "surf travel" search intent. Worth monitoring.
